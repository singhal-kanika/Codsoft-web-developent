About Navratri

Navratri is a significant Hindu festival celebrated across India and various parts of the world. The word "Navratri" means "nine nights" in Sanskrit, and during these nine nights, devotees worship the goddess Durga, who represents power and strength.

The festival marks the victory of Goddess Durga over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. Each day of Navratri is dedicated to one of the nine forms of Durga, known as Navdurga, and devotees observe rituals, fasts, and prayers to seek her blessings.

One of the most vibrant aspects of Navratri is the traditional dance forms of Garba and Dandiya Raas, performed in large gatherings. People dress in colorful clothes, play rhythmic music, and dance in a circle with sticks (dandiya), celebrating unity and joy.

Navratri culminates on the tenth day, known as Dussehra or Vijayadashami, symbolizing Lord Rama's victory over Ravana, another celebration of the victory of good over evil.

The festival brings communities together and promotes devotion, cultural heritage, and the celebration of life and positivity.
